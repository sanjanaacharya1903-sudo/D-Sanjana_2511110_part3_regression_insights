# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

Regression analysis was performed to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales across retail stores. Both simple and multiple linear regression models were evaluated. The multiple regression model was selected because it explains approximately **83%** of the variation in monthly sales and provides the most comprehensive business insights.

---

## Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

The analysis indicates that the following variables have the strongest positive association with monthly sales:

1. **Marketing Spend**

   * Higher marketing investment is associated with increased monthly sales.
   * Stores investing more in promotions generally attract more customers and generate higher revenue.

2. **Customer Footfall**

   * Footfall is one of the strongest predictors of sales.
   * Increased store visits generally translate into greater sales performance.

3. **Inventory Availability**

   * Maintaining high inventory availability prevents stock-outs and supports higher sales.
   * Well-stocked stores consistently perform better.

Customer rating also contributes positively but has a comparatively smaller impact after accounting for other business variables.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Average discount percentage and some regional or store-type differences showed weaker statistical evidence after controlling for multiple variables. These factors may influence sales in specific situations but should not be viewed as primary drivers without additional analysis.

Regression identifies relationships but does not prove that these variables directly cause changes in sales.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

Management should focus on the following actions:

* Increase marketing investment in high-potential stores.
* Improve customer footfall through promotional campaigns and loyalty programs.
* Maintain high inventory availability to reduce lost sales opportunities.
* Monitor customer satisfaction to support long-term revenue growth.
* Continuously review store performance using regression models as new data becomes available.

---

## Risks and Limitations

Several limitations should be considered:

* Regression demonstrates association rather than causation.
* External economic conditions were not included.
* Seasonal demand and promotional events may influence sales.
* Local competition and customer demographics were not explicitly modeled.
* Future performance may differ if market conditions change.

---

## Conclusion

The multiple regression model provides valuable business insights for improving monthly sales. Marketing spend, customer footfall, and inventory availability appear to be the most important business drivers. Leadership should prioritize these factors while recognizing that regression models support decision-making but should not be interpreted as proof of cause-and-effect relationships.
