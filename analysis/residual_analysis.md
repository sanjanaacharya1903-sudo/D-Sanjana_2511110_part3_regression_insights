# Residual Analysis

## Overview

Residual analysis was performed using the final multiple regression model to evaluate prediction accuracy.

Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales

Positive residuals indicate that actual sales were higher than predicted, while negative residuals indicate that actual sales were lower than predicted.

---

## Largest Positive Residuals

The following observations had the largest positive residuals:

| Observation | Interpretation                                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Record 18   | Sales exceeded model expectations, possibly due to successful local promotions. |
| Record 47   | Exceptional store performance not fully explained by the selected variables.    |
| Record 96   | Higher-than-expected customer demand.                                           |
| Record 184  | Seasonal or regional factors may have increased sales.                          |
| Record 251  | Strong local management or customer loyalty may have contributed.               |

These stores outperformed model predictions, suggesting additional factors not included in the regression model.

---

## Largest Negative Residuals

The following observations had the largest negative residuals:

| Observation | Interpretation                                                      |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Record 31   | Sales were considerably lower than expected.                        |
| Record 72   | Possible operational issues or temporary disruptions.               |
| Record 138  | Lower customer demand than predicted.                               |
| Record 219  | Inventory shortages or staffing issues may have reduced sales.      |
| Record 304  | Local competition or external events may have affected performance. |

These stores underperformed compared with model expectations.

---

## Business Interpretation

Positive residuals indicate stores that are performing better than expected based on their marketing, inventory, customer traffic, and other measured variables. Management should investigate these stores to identify best practices that can be replicated elsewhere.

Negative residuals indicate stores performing below expectations. These stores may require operational reviews, inventory improvements, staffing adjustments, or local marketing initiatives.

---

## Model Performance

Overall, residuals appear reasonably balanced around zero, suggesting that the regression model provides an acceptable fit for the data. However, a few large residuals indicate that additional explanatory variables could further improve prediction accuracy.

---

## Conclusion

The residual analysis supports the selection of the multiple regression model. Although most observations are predicted reasonably well, unusual store-specific factors remain unexplained. Future models could include seasonal trends, local economic conditions, competitor intensity, and promotional events to improve predictive performance.
